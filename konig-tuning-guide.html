Konig Tuning Guide: Turbo, Gears & Suspension

The Konig is your pride and joy. But in its stock form, it's slow, heavy, and handles like a boat. To dominate the street races, you need to upgrade everything from the intake to the exhaust. This guide covers the essential upgrade path.

ADVERTISEMENT

Stage 1: The Essentials

Before you spend $2,000 on a turbo, fix the basics.

  • Tires: Stock tires are trash. Buy Sport Tires immediately for grip.
  • Weight Reduction: Remove the spare tire and passenger seats. It's free speed.
  • Differential:
    • Welded Diff: Cheap, wheels lock together. Best for Drifting.
    • LSD (Limited Slip): Expensive. Best for Racing/Cornering.

Stage 2: Turbo & ECU (Forced Induction)

The biggest power jump. You can buy the full kit at Roger's Parts Store.

Required Parts

Turbo Manifold + Turbocharger + Intercooler + Piping + ECU.

*Warning: You MUST install the ECU to tune the fuel mix, otherwise the engine may blow.

Tuning Air/Fuel Ratio (AFR)

Use a screwdriver on the Carburetor/ECU screw while the engine is running.

AFR Value Result
11.0 - 12.0 Rich (Safe, but wasting fuel)
12.5 - 13.5 Optimal Power (Target)
15.0+ Lean (Overheating Risk)

Suspension & Stance

Install Coilovers to adjust height and camber.

  • Drift Setup: Front negative camber (-3.0 to -5.0), stiff rear suspension.
  • Grip Setup: Mild camber (-1.0 to -2.0), softer damping for bumps.
